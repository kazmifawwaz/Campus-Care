# Campus-Care
CampusCare is a web based appointment management tool specifically designed for university clinic environments. The application helps solve the critical challenge of healthcare accessibility for university students and staff within higher education institutions
Our Team Members Worked on this project,
KANJAK MEMON, Syed Fawwaz Kazmi, Ibrahim Shuaib Wyne, Hussain Shaheer
SDG: SDG 3 - Good Health and Well-Being
University students are facing many mental health issues, including:
41% of university students suffer from moderate to severe anxiety
36% of university students suffer from depression
60% of students in need of mental health services do not seek help due to inaccessibility of services
Summary of SDLC Work CampusCare Project.
Our four-week experience of creating CampusCare was based on an organized Agile Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) methodology, and was broken into five major parts in the Scrum methodology with two one-week sprints. In Week 1 (Planning Phase), we organised our five-member Scrum team, and our team role assignments, chose SDG 3: Good Health and Well-Being as our area of focus and thoroughly justified it, prepared our Product Vision document, analysed stakeholders, and established our board on Trello and a product backlog consisting of 18 items in it and a properly developed GitHub repository with a proper folder structure (/planning, /design, /testing, /final). The Requirements Analysis Phase (Week 1-2) was a phase that required 12 detailed user stories to be developed using the format of "As a... I want... so that..." format, ranking them by the MoSCoW technique, and developing appropriate UML notation both the Use Case and Class Diagrams as a model of system functionality and data relationships. During Week 2 (Design Phase), we used UI sketches and digital wireframes created in Figma to convert requirements into visual design, created complete user flows among students, doctors, and administrators, and held design reviews to be on track with user stories. Week 3 (Prototype Development Phase) was dedicated to the creation of high-fidelity clickable prototype and full user journeys of all three types of users, including the responsive design of both mobile and desktop interfaces. Testing and Evaluation (Week 3-4) consisted of developing a thorough test plan, having two usability tests with actual student users, having 23 feedback points and bugs to track in Trello with priority labels, fixing the major issues, and confirming this with second-round testing-95 percent of the tasks complete and a 4.6/5 user satisfaction. We were very disciplined in the use of Trello throughout the entire SDLC, frequently taking board snapshots on a weekly basis, burndown charts displaying sprint completion, and cards at To Do - Doing - Review - Done stages with valid assignments and due dates. The meaningful commit messages such as the addition of the first user story, the uploading of wire frames to design the UI, and the addition of a prototype evaluation feedback were added to our GitHub repository and provided a comprehensive timeline of how our project evolved to become the finished product.
